# Principal Qualificand in Verbal Cognition

**Author:** Tapas Khanra  
**Journal:** Journal of the Oriental Institute  
**Year:** 2025  
**Pages:** 20  

## Abstract

A comprehension extracted from sentence meaning which has syntactico-semantic relations between word meanings is known as verbal cognition. In such verbal cognition, among many meaningful units one is considered the **principal qualificand (mukhyaviśeṣya)**, while other elements are linked to it directly or indirectly.

According to the logicians, the nominative meaning is the principal qualificand. However, the Sanskrit grammatical tradition disagrees. Grammarians maintain that the **action denoted by the verbal root** functions as the principal qualificand in sentence meaning (vākyārtha).

Using evidence from the *Mahābhāṣya* and analyzing sentences such as:

- *paśya mṛgo dhāvati*
- *pacatibhavati*

this paper argues that the grammatical tradition provides a stronger explanation.

The paper further analyzes relations using technical concepts such as:

- prakāratā
- viśeṣyatā

and proposes a structural representation identifying the principal qualificand both at the **sentence level** and **discourse level**.
